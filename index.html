The youngster and the redfaced girl turn aside up the bushy hill, The jour printer with gray head and gaunt jaws works at his case, These were dispatched with bayonets or battered with the blunts of muskets; . . . . A call in the midst of the crowd, The pedlar sweats with his pack on his back—the purchaser higgles about the odd cent. I dote on myself…there is that lot of me, and all so luscious, A word of reality…materialism first and last imbueing. Workmen searching after damages and to make indispensible repairs, To niches aside and junior bending. I effuse my flesh in eddies and drift it in lacy jags..
The nearest gnat is an explanation and a drop or the motion of waves a key, And the night is for you and me and all, The sentries desert every other part of me. And mine a word of the modern…a word en masse. The ring of alarm-bells…the cry of fire…the whirr of swift-streaking engines and hose-carts with premonitory tinkles and colored lights, But they are not the Me myself. If I worship any particular thing it shall be some of the spread of my body; Where the alligator in his tough pimples sleeps by the bayou.
